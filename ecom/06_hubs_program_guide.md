# How to Host a BitGN Hub
**BitGN challenge hubs**
**Audience:** community organizers, local partners  
**Version:** 2.0
**Last updated:** 26 May 2026 (Vienna time)


## What a hub is
A hub is a local, in-person meetup for people participating in a BitGN challenge. The same hub profile can host several BitGN challenges over time without being recreated.

BitGN challenges run globally online. Communities can self-organize local on-site hubs (cafes, coworking spaces, universities) to collaborate in person while participating in the same global challenge.

Everyone still competes in the same global challenge and submits to the same platform. The hub gives people in your city a place to build together. Local hub leaderboards are also available for supported challenges.

Vienna is listed as the "HQ hub" simply because the organizer is based there. Hubs can happen anywhere.

## What BitGN provides to hubs
- a public hub listing on the BitGN website (approval-based: only approved hubs are shown)
- a hub page you can manage and reuse across several challenges
- separate challenge participation rows for current, upcoming, and past challenges
- a challenge-specific hub check-in code / QR for attribution
- local hub leaderboards, based on check-ins

## What hubs must provide
To host a hub, you're responsible for:
- a venue + estimated capacity
- a public event or registration link for each challenge you host
- attendee registration, reminders, and local communication (you manage registration, not BitGN)
- on challenge day: share the right hub QR/code for check-in so attendees can attribute to your hub for that challenge
- basic local logistics + safety readiness
- optional: local sponsors/prizes (hub-managed)

## How to create a hub (step by step)
1. Log into BitGN and go to your profile page.
2. Add a city where you want to host a hub.
3. Click "I want to host a hub".
4. Fill in the hub form:
   - hub name
   - city/location
   - hub link where you accept registrations (should contain a link back to the competition page at BitGN)
   - venue size (example: "10-20 people")
   - optional hub description
   - confirm the required rules
5. Submit. Your hub starts as pending while BitGN reviews it.

After approval, your hub becomes public on the BitGN site. You can come back later to edit hub-level details. If you edit an approved hub, BitGN reviews the changes, and the public page keeps showing the last approved version until the update is approved.

## Naming and branding
Use a clear local name for the hub, such as "BitGN Hub in <City>" or a community name that makes the city and host context obvious.

For a specific challenge event, use a challenge-specific title that includes BitGN, the challenge name, and the city.

Please don't present your hub as globally sponsored or "officially hosted" by BitGN, or describe your hub as a partner, co-organizer, or official organizer of BitGN.
Hubs participate as independent local hosts providing a venue for participation, and all hubs operate on equal footing.

## How to host another challenge at the same hub
Use your existing hub profile. From your profile, open the hub and choose the action to host a new challenge.

Submit the challenge-specific details: challenge, registration link, venue size if changed, and any short event note. BitGN reviews this separately. Your public hub page can then show several challenge rows while keeping one durable hub identity.

## What happens after review
- Approved hubs are visible on the BitGN site.
- People who have tagged the venue city as a city of interest will see the hub in their profiles.
- Approved challenge participations appear on the hub page as current, upcoming, or past.
- Public hub pages may show event links and leaderboard links, but they do not show raw join codes or QR secrets.
- The host/profile surface shows the challenge-specific QR/code for each approved challenge participation.

## How hub check-in works
Each challenge has its own hub QR/code. Share the right QR/code with attendees for that challenge.

When a participant scans the QR/code, BitGN attributes that participant to your hub for that challenge. A participant can have different hubs in different challenges, but only one active hub per challenge at a time.

## How hub leaderboards work
Participants appear on the global leaderboard and, where supported, on hub-filtered leaderboard views for the relevant challenge.

Main challenge windows for different BitGN competitions do not overlap. After the main challenge window closes, the Hall of Fame or frozen result stays historically fixed, while the live leaderboard can remain open for ongoing runs, late experimentation, and continued hub activity.

Live hub leaderboard views use the participant's current active hub for that challenge. Frozen or Hall of Fame views preserve the hub attribution that existed at freeze time; later joins should not rewrite historical results.

## Non-negotiables
To be listed as an official BitGN hub, you must agree to:
- enforce Code of Conduct
- non-discrimination
- safety and legality
- **no charging participants for competition participation** - Optional venue cost-sharing for food/drinks/etc. is fine, but competition participation must be free.

## Data boundary
BitGN does not share platform registration data with hubs. Hubs manage their own attendee lists and recruiting processes independently.

## Responsibility and liability
BitGN provides the platform and hub listing. Your hub event is your responsibility as the local organizer. This includes handling on-site incidents and escalating Code of Conduct reports when needed.

## Local prizes (optional)
Hubs may offer local prizes for hub participants or local winners (hub-managed). Any local prizes or sponsors are not sponsorship of the global BitGN challenge and should be clearly presented as hub-local only.

## Sponsor neutrality
Local sponsors can be shown on hub-controlled materials/pages, but they do not get promotion on the global BitGN platform.
